Additional Details:

Purpose: Establish primary emphasis on luxurious, long-haul narrow-body flights with additional Economy seating based on demand.

Main configurations for most aircrafts consist of all three classes: Economy, Business, and First Class.
Emphasis on luxury with Economy Class seats added.

Long-haul flights consist of mainly small but not limited to large airports within its capabilities.

Advantages: A faster aircraft (987 kph) capable of handling more passengers than the DC-9-10 (230 pax - single class configuration). Better usage of fuel for high density flights. Low maintenance (400 flight hours before A-Check).

Disadvantages: High fuel consumption (19 lbs/km). The aircraft can be replaced with bigger wide-bodies to handle long-haul routes with more passenger capacity while using an equivalent number of fuel consumption.

Roughly ~$6,000,000 per aircraft * 60 total MC-21-400s = ~$180,000,000 spent on the MC-21-400 fleet.

Return on Investment (ROI) Status: Positive
